Documentation & Methodology
1. Public Subsidies Framework
Public funding tracking is split across two fundamentally different accounting and legislative frameworks to capture the evolution of Spain's solar policy.
•	Feed-in Tariffs / System Premiums: * Source: Comisión Nacional de los Mercados y la Competencia (CNMC), specifically historical annual macro reports and the Liquidaciones definitivas de la tasa del sector eléctrico / régimen especial (Final Settlements of the Special Regime).
•	Methodology: This column tracks net annual disbursements paid out to operational solar plants through the national grid settlement system. It encompasses early fixed-price feed-in tariffs (under RD 436/2004 and RD 661/2007), as well as the compressed payouts distributed via the post-2014 "Reasonable Return" standard (RD 413/2014). It represents true sovereign system cost liabilities rather than temporary budget allocations.
•	EU & Capital Grants: * Source: Spain's Ministry for Ecological Transition (MITECO), Instituto para la Diversificación y Ahorro de la Energía (IDAE), and the European Commission's NextGenerationEU recovery dashboard tracking.
•	Methodology: Represents direct capital injections, mapping historical local regional grants from 2006 to 2020 and shifting strictly to the drawdowns of the Plan de Recuperación, Transformación y Resiliencia (PRTR) after 2021 (primarily driven by RD 477/2021 incentive structures for rooftop solar self-consumption and battery installations).
2. Private Solar PV Capital Investment
Private capital metrics represent standard tracking of Capital Expenditures (CAPEX) mapping private market growth over the 20-year span.
•	Annual Capacity Added (MW):
•	Source: Reconciled from annual market updates published by UNEF (Unión Española Fotovoltaica) and verified against actual grid-interconnection registries tracked by Spain's transmission operator, Red Eléctrica (REE).
•	Estimated Average CAPEX (€/kW) & Total Investment (€m):
•	Source: BloombergNEF (BNEF) Global PV Market Outlooks, Lazard Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE) standard historical tables for Southern Europe, and institutional project financing benchmarks inside Spain.
•	Methodology: The total private capital footprint uses a standard capacity-to-asset multiplier model:

‭$$\text{Total Private Capital Investment (€m)} = \frac{\text{Annual Capacity Added (MW)} \times \text{Estimated Avg CAPEX (€/kW)}}{1,000}$$‬‭‬

CAPEX numbers capture weighted market averages per year. The index drops from extreme premium luxury module rates in 2006 (€6,200/kW) down to highly efficient scale economics by 2025 (€700/kW). Fluctuations in the 2022 timeframe mirror a dramatic internal mix shift: though industrial utility utility costs were dropping, Spain added unprecedented percentages of smaller, more expensive residential rooftop systems that artificially pulled up the macro average blended cost for that calendar year.
